# iOS App Icon Optimization

The icon is not just branding. It is the first conversion asset most people see.

## Core Rule

If the icon does not read instantly at small size, it is not ready.

## What Strong iOS Icons Usually Do

- rely on one dominant symbol or shape
- use strong contrast without becoming noisy
- stay recognizable in both light and dark contexts
- feel native on the home screen and distinctive in the App Store
- connect clearly to the app's in-product visual system

## Review In This Order

1. small-size clarity
2. silhouette and recognizability
3. contrast in light and dark surroundings
4. distinctiveness versus category competitors
5. consistency with app branding and screenshots

## Good Icon Heuristics

- one mark beats a scene
- simple geometry beats tiny detail
- bold color direction beats muddy gradients
- the icon should be describable in a few words
- the exported art should assume Apple applies masking

## Competitive Judgment

- study the top apps in your category before exploring concepts
- match category trust signals but avoid looking interchangeable
- if the category is crowded with blue minimal icons, find a cleaner but more memorable contrast

## Avoid

- text inside the icon
- too many objects or layers
- weak center focus
- raw bitmap edges or backgrounds that break when masked
- designing only on a large artboard and never testing at realistic size
